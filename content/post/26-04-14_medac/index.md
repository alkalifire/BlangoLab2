Our publication on a tRNA hypomodification influencing 5-fluorocytosine resistance, published in _Nucleic Acids Research_ in 2024, was honored with the medac research prize!

Awarded annually by the Leibniz-HKI in partnership with the pharmaceutical company _medac GmbH_, this prize celebrates outstanding collaborative and interdisciplinary research that addresses complex questions in infection biology. Alongside three other groups at the HKI, we shared this esteemed prize.

Matt, alongside the first authors of this study, Alex and Valentina, graciously accepted the prize. This recognition motivates us to continue our quest in advancing fungal RNA biology in the context of infection.

Props to Valentina who presented a summary of the study at the ceremony and made us all proud.



Honored study:

__Bruch A__, __Lazarova V__, Berg M, Krueger T, Schaeuble S, Kelani AA, Mertens B, Lehenberger P, Kniemeyer O, Kaiser S, Panagiotou G, Gsaller F, Blango MG (2024) tRNA hypomodification facilitates 5-fluorocytosine resistance via cross-pathway control system activation in _Aspergillus fumigatus_. _Nucleic Acids Research_, https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/epdf/10.1002/anie.202417220
